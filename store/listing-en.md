## Full description (max 4000 chars)
"What if the Sun suddenly disappeared — where would the Earth fly off to?"
"What if Jupiter became a star — what happens to the Solar System?"

What-If Space Lab is a hands-on laboratory where you test these cosmic
"what-ifs" with real physics — a true N-body gravity simulation. Grab a
planet with your finger and move it, change the Sun's mass, swap planets
with each other... and watch the universe react for real.

■ Experiments you can run
・☀️ What if the Sun vanished? (law of inertia)
・⭐ What if Jupiter became a star? (binary systems)
・🛑 What if the Earth stopped orbiting? (free fall)
・🔥 What if the Earth were at Mercury's distance? (Kepler's laws)
・⚖️ What if the Sun were twice / half its mass? (escape velocity)
...and more — 8+ "what-if" experiments in total.

■ Free-play / research mode
Freely change the size, mass and distance of planets, then watch how the
Solar System falls apart. See planets swallowed by the Sun or flung out
into deep space — each event comes with a plain-language explanation of
"why it happened."

■ Observation mode (bonus)
・🌌 The rotation of the Milky Way (±3 billion years)
・🌠 The history of the universe, from the Big Bang to 23.8 billion years
・🔬 The microscopic moment atoms are born (cosmic dawn — 380,000 years)

From space-loving teens to curious adults, this is like a hands-on lab
notebook for experiencing "why?" No difficult equations required. Just
touch it — and try breaking the universe.

※ This app simulates Newtonian gravity (the N-body problem). It does not
model tidal disruption, relativistic effects, and so on. It is an
approximate simulation intended for learning and play.
